Human Conversation seed deck expert-pass ledger
Date: 2026-07-05

Communication job:
By the end, a seed investor should believe Human Conversation can become a venture-scale operating system for real-world communities because it starts with one measurable club loop, captures proprietary relationship signal, and turns that signal into repeatable software.

Pass 1 - Garry Tan / YC seed lens
Critique: The current deck opens with a strong category line but takes too long to prove what the product does in one concrete loop.
Deck action: Move the wedge and product loop earlier. Make the product answer: who needs a conversation, why now, what to ask, what happens next.

Pass 2 - Andy Raskin strategic narrative lens
Critique: The old game/new game needs to be explicit. Old game is more software, feeds, dashboards, and calendars. New game is AI moving computer work into the background so human conversation becomes the interface.
Deck action: Add a category slide built around "Not another chat app" and "the product gets people back in the room."

Pass 3 - April Dunford positioning lens
Critique: The category cannot sound like a beautiful nonprofit thesis. It needs a buyer-use case: community builders need a sales engine for relationship-led growth.
Deck action: Put "sales engine" into the cover/subcopy and business-model slide, while keeping human-first language intact.

Pass 4 - Reid Hoffman network-effects lens
Critique: The deck needs to show what compounds. A conversation note alone is not a moat. A returned player, better invite, new host, referral, and next call are the compounding loop.
Deck action: Add a dedicated compounding-graph slide after the data moat.

Pass 5 - Sequoia seed partner lens
Critique: Seed investors need to see wedge, proof, business model, team, use of funds, and why this can expand beyond one club. The 12-slide version had some of these, but the live deck's 18-slide spine explains them better.
Deck action: Move to a 14-slide seed deck with separate business model, founder-market fit, and seed plan slides.

Pass 6 - Synthetic skeptical seed investor
Critique: Any unsupported adoption rate, exact acceptance rate, external-club proof, or current utilization claim would weaken credibility.
Deck action: Keep aggregate live-lab counts and planning-model utilization economics explicitly sourced and caveated. Do not add unverified adoption-rate proof.

Pass 7 - Synthetic club-operator buyer
Critique: The deck must make a club operator feel the work: someone drifted, staff knows why, the next invitation is better, and the club makes money from the same courts.
Deck action: Reframe the wedge around one person drifting and keep the NEPC business impact slide.

Pass 8 - Synthetic product operator
Critique: The product needs to feel buildable, not magical. It is a queue, context, capture fields, action execution, and scorecard.
Deck action: Make the product-loop slide concrete and use the seed plan to name memory, recommendations, actions, and reporting.

Pass 9 - Synthetic CFO / data-room auditor
Critique: The deck has to separate verified proof, planning model, and next proof package. Investors forgive early numbers when the proof hierarchy is honest.
Deck action: Use 9,226 booking records, 1,273 invitation-court records, 174 weekly booking rhythm, and 52-62% utilization gap only as sourced 2025 aggregate operating signal. Keep 55% -> 80% / $500K / $764K as planning model, not final underwriting.

Pass 10 - Synthetic category designer / story editor
Critique: The strongest phrases are already in the live HTML deck. The PPTX should learn from it instead of paraphrasing into weaker language.
Deck action: Pull forward "Technology got between us," "Not another chat app," "The wedge is one person drifting," "Every return makes the graph smarter," and "Back the operating system for real-world social communities."

Pass 11 - Steve Jobs product-demo clarity lens
Critique: A great pitch makes the product feel obvious in one concrete moment. The deck should show the system hearing a human outcome and turning it into an action.
Deck action: Add the live-deck product-interface beat: heard signal, staff command, AI action.

Pass 12 - Synthetic product-demo investor
Critique: Without a product-demo slide, the story risks reading as services plus brand. A seed investor needs to see the repeatable workflow that becomes software.
Deck action: Insert a tangible "conversation updates the system" slide between product loop and proof.

Pass 13 - Live deck source-of-truth pass
Critique: The live GitHub Pages deck is the source Dave explicitly named. A stale alternate read introduced copy that no-cache verification did not confirm.
Deck action: Reconcile the PPTX back to the verified current live page: "Technology got between us," "Not another chat app," "The wedge is one person drifting," "The moat is what members only tell a human," "Every return makes the graph smarter," and "Back the operating system for real-world social communities."

Pass 14 - Synthetic final-partner pass
Critique: The deck should read as a seed company, not a social mission deck: painful missing signal, concrete product loop, operating lab, proprietary human signal, business model, founder-market fit, and seed use.
Deck action: Keep the deck at 15 slides, preserve source caveats, add no unsupported numbers, and keep the concrete product-demo slide.

Pass 15 - Nancy Duarte narrative clarity lens
Critique: The deck's middle needed to make the product workflow obvious without presenter narration. The live page does this better by walking through person, context, action, and human-interface moments.
Deck action: Strengthen the wedge and product-loop copy with live-page specifics: came once then vanished, schedule/confidence/fit, next action, saved memory, and seed use around missed-return, invite, group, and leader loops.

Pass 16 - Synthetic top-tier seed investor
Critique: The deck explains the company, but the close needs to make the seed round feel underwriteable: what capital proves, what gets built, and what expansion milestone follows.
Deck action: Tighten the business-impact caveat into a seed proof target and turn the final slide into three concrete use-of-seed milestones: prove live loops, build the software layer, expand beyond the first club.
